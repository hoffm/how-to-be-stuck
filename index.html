the attitude

But…
Contempt without assistance.
A link to a page you don’t understand, with no explanation.
Overly brief assistance, with or without contempt.
Why do people do this?
They're busy.
They've answered your question before.
It's super easy to look up the answer--for them.
They've paid their dues
They're having a bad day. They're mean.
This attitude stinks. But...

RTFM:
the advice



You should RTM.


You should do your research.


Don't be lazy!

Strugling is valuable.
Don't be lazy; you're doing yourself a disservice.
If someone is helping you to learn for yourself, that's great.
So why is good advice spoiled with bad attitude?
People don't get how hard it is to be new.
Out: Why is it hard? Plato.

“And how will you inquire into a thing when you are wholly ignorant of what it is?

Even if you happen to bump right into it, how will you know it is the thing you didn’t know?”


Plato, Meno, 80d1-4

(Virtue.) Leaving aside it's merits as a philosophical argument...
It's illustrative for us.
How does this apply to searching for answers on the Internet.

What's the question?

What's the jargon?

Whom do you ask?

What are they saying?

How do you do that?

Did it work?

What question do you ask? What's relevant?
Just because your answer is out there, doesn't mean you know how to find it.
Even if you find it, you may not recognize it.
Even if you think you have the answer, you may not know how to check that it's the answer.
To summarize:

You need to learn stuff to know stuff.

You need to know stuff to learn stuff.

You need background knowledge and research skills.
How do you get them?
They come with time and pain.
You will spend whole days where you "got nothing done" (AFAIK, this doesn't ever end)
These are valuable.
